Man Vs. Machine: Fullscreen Works Smarter, Not Harder

The world of online video is an increasingly competitive market. When the burdgeoning online video company Fullscreen started in the spring of 2011, it decided to spend smarter instead of work harder, setting out with Heroku to build a product that would do more than the 200+ person sales staff of its competitors.

Why Waste Time Wearing Too Many Hats?

Fullscreen is a next-generation digital media company that powers multi-channel YouTube networks and helps content creators and brand marketers build audiences and community. Many of its competitors have full-time employees to handle running the servers and keep everything on the back-end running smoothly. Fullscreen, however, quickly built and deployed three unique products in less than eight month’s time because it didn’t have to waste precious time and energy worrying about handling its inevitable growth. For CTO Drew Baumann, Heroku was the obvious choice because it allowed him to focus on his area of expertise and let Heroku’s platform handle the details.

“Since Heroku is synonymous with quick deployment and dependable hosting it was a no brainer for me,” explained Baumann. “Of course the non-technical people asked why we don’t just buy servers and host ourselves, but I simply explained that Heroku fit our philosophy of building product quickly. When you are in a startup you are forced to wear multiple hats. I saw the opportunity to take off the sys admin hat and also save the company money and time, our most valuable asset in an increasingly competitive space.”

Iterate, Iterate, Iterate

For Fullscreen VP of product Jay Stakelon, speed was important not only during the initial product development cycle, but also in fine tuning the product to meet the users’ needs as they arise.

“Speed is truly of the essence. If we don’t do it today, someone else is going to do it tomorrow. Heroku, enables us to build, test and ship faster than we ever would be able to otherwise," said Stakelon. “Getting things out there quickly, seeing what works, seeing what doesn’t work and being able to double down on the things that really deliver value to our users – that’s what’s going to make or break our product’s success.”

How does Heroku make it easy to iterate on new ideas? Baumann points to the frictionless nature of Heroku’s Add-ons, of which Fullscreen uses more than a dozen.

“Heroku makes it so easy to plug and play,” said Baumann. “The app needs email? Sendgrid. You need SSL? No problem with SSL piggybacking. At the end of the day the ‘Ah ha!’s really comes from the efficiency Heroku provides as a platform for hosting web apps.”

If You Build It, They Will Come…But Will You Be Ready?

A big part of Heroku’s value comes not only in rapid development and deployment, but also the ability to quickly scale to your app’s needs. A little over a year ago, Fullscreen was simply an idea. Today, it handles more than 50,000 internal API calls daily, with a monthly growth of more than 400 YouTube channels and a 35% monthly increase in users. Being able to stay up and running smoothly, without the distraction of running the servers and handling issues like load balancing, has allowed Fullscreen to enjoy a monthly increase in revenue it might not enjoy otherwise.

“We ingest a massive amount of data into our system on a fairly regular basis," noted Stakelon. "When we run a campaign, we’re processing data every hour. Being able to use workers and custom processes is really important to being able to scale up and down. That flexibility comes in handy during those times when we really need a lot more horsepower.”

Were Fullscreen running its own servers, scaling to handle traffic increases would require the know-how to use ever-changing, complicated scaling solutions. With Heroku, scaling is as simple as a single command line in a terminal. For Fullscreen, not having to handle the difficulties of scaling has meant days, if not weeks, of saved time and energy.

“When you appoint a developer to handle a server issue, they typically scramble the web to find the appropriate solution. It’s not an effective use of time and it’s expensive," said Baumann. "It’s really a blessing that Heroku takes care of that for us.”
